## Approach

A single leg was removed from a dry-pinned beetle collected in the 1980s. DNA
was extracted with a silica-column protocol adapted for degraded material
(extended proteinase K digestion, low-volume elution). A tagmentation-based
library was sequenced to ~2× nuclear coverage.

## Outcome

- Complete mitochondrial genome assembled.
- Nuclear ribosomal cluster (18S/28S) recovered at usable depth.
- Fragment lengths peaked ~90 bp, consistent with dry storage degradation.

## Caveats

Endogenous yield varies strongly with specimen handling history. Non-destructive
sampling of a single leg was sufficient here but may not be for smaller taxa.
