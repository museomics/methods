## Approach

A small toe-pad clip was taken from a study skin. DNA was extracted with a
magnetic-bead protocol optimised for short fragments, and a single-stranded
library was built to capture ultra-short and damaged molecules. Shallow shotgun
sequencing followed.

## Outcome

- Complete mitogenome at high coverage.
- Sufficient nuclear data for SNP-based identification.
- Deamination profile consistent with historical DNA, useful for authentication.

## Caveats

Single-stranded library prep is more costly and laborious than double-stranded
approaches; reserve it for the most degraded material where it pays off.
